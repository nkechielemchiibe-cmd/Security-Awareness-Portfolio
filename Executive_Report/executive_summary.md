 Executive Summary

This report provides a high‑level overview of the Security Awareness Program designed to strengthen human‑factor security across the healthcare organization. The program focuses on reducing risk, improving staff behavior, and supporting compliance with privacy and security regulations.

 Program Purpose
To build a resilient security culture by equipping staff with the knowledge, tools, and behaviors needed to protect patient data and clinical systems.

 Key Components of the Program
1. **Program Strategy**
   - Defines the vision, goals, and governance model for the awareness program.
   - Aligns security culture initiatives with organizational priorities.

2. **Phishing Simulations**
   - Beginner, intermediate, and advanced scenarios.
   - Measures susceptibility to phishing attacks and improves reporting behavior.

3. **Training Materials**
   - Posters and micro‑learning content on passwords, phishing, and PHI protection.
   - Designed for clinical and non‑clinical environments.

4. **Incident Response Playbooks**
   - Standardized procedures for phishing, lost devices, and unauthorized access.
   - Ensures consistent, rapid, and compliant response to human‑factor incidents.

5. **Security Culture Dashboard**
   - Power BI dashboard tracking key metrics such as phishing failure rate, MFA adoption, and training completion.
   - Supports data‑driven decision making for leadership.

 Program Outcomes
- Increased staff awareness and reporting of suspicious activity.
- Reduced phishing simulation failure rates over time.
- Improved compliance with training and password hygiene.
- Faster and more consistent incident response.
- Stronger alignment between security, clinical operations, and leadership.

 Executive Recommendations
- Continue monthly phishing simulations and targeted training.
- Expand dashboard metrics to include department‑level trends.
- Strengthen MFA enforcement across all clinical and administrative systems.
- Integrate awareness metrics into quarterly leadership reviews.
- Maintain ongoing communication campaigns to reinforce secure behaviors.

 Conclusion
This Security Awareness Program provides a comprehensive, scalable, and measurable approach to reducing human‑factor risk in a healthcare environment. It supports regulatory compliance, protects patient information, and strengthens the organization’s overall security posture.
